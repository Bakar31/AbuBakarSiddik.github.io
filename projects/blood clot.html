Blood Clot Origins Classification in Ischemic Stroke

  • Tech Stack: Python, Deep Learning, Transfer Learning, CNN, EfficentNet, Tensorflow
  • Github URL: Project Link

Motivation: Stroke remains the second-leading cause of death worldwide. Each year in the United States, over 700,000 individuals experience an ischemic stroke caused by a blood clot blocking an artery to the brain.

Goal: The goal is to classify the blood clot origins in ischemic stroke. Using whole slide digital pathology images, the model should be able to differentiates between the two major acute ischemic stroke (AIS) etiology subtypes: cardiac and large artery atherosclerosis.

My Approch:

  • Open slide + CNN: Read data using open slide and train the model on them.
  • Convert large .tif files to PNG + CNN: Digital pathology images are huge and need a huge amount of memory. So I converted them first into PNG format and then applied CNN.
  • EfficientNet: I utilized EfficentNetB5 for final model traning.
  • Ensemble

    • Best approache: First, I converted the high-quality tif files into PNG format to reduce memory consumption, then used EfficentNetB5 architecture to train the model with these images.
